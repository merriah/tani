When you don't hear the wings at night

When you don't hear wings at night
and eyes
seeing--nothing
but overloaded with task
deciphering ten thousand
shades of can't see enough
taking the anemic
messages
from apologetic eyeballs
somehow passing off
the bits of sign
to the ears or,
for chrissakes, the nose,
tallying
a report card. The mental
headscratch follows.

you think
it must have been an owl
because I couldn't see it
and I didn't hear
the wings.



Sing

small fish
foraging the sunlit
shallows
of this pond.
a heron.

wind coming no lower
than treetops.

if i could show you
the perfection
of circles
within circles

dragonflying her muse.
sing.

A reflection

I
curl
and uncurl
my eyes
upon black spires and limb
formed twisted
under a life sentence of sun wind heat cold.

life
forces
us to live out and in
days without respite
and we break on our shore
arrested by beauty
the echo remains, briefly, and passes.
